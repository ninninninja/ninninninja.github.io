Phase separation in biological systems

Phase separation is a common physical phenomenon. During the past decade, many researches have proved it takes part in cellular programs such as stress response, metabolisms and development. Personally, I would like to divide the phase separation processes in cell into two groups, liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) and liquid-to-solid phase separation. Although the formation of the both processes rely on the similar mechanism, the properties are quite different and can contribute to different biological programs.

Liquid-liquid phase separation

The LLPS in definition is that a group of molecules do not dissolve into solvent and form spherical shape of droplets without membrane. Interestingly, these droplets still maintain the feature of liquid that means the droplets can diffuse, dissolve and aggregate in the liquid phase. Biologically, the well-known examples including P bodies, stress granules and Cajal body mainly consist of protein and mRNA which is capable of carrying out reactions in confined spaces. Take P bodies for example. The P-bodies (Processing bodies) locate in cytoplasmic region which are charge of repression of mRNA and mRNA decay. In fact, there are several ways that can promote the formation of LLPS. Firstly, the protein-RNA aggregation is the most common molecular mechanism for LLPS. Relying on the positively charged amino acid sequences and negatively charged RNA, the binding helps the molecular aggregations “escape” from dissolving into solvent. RGG domain and intrinsic disordered sequences are widely researched targets. Secondly, the base pairing between RNA such as CAG repeats has been reported to phase separate with 46 times repeat in vivo and in vitro which can be disrupted by adding anti-sense RNA. Thirdly, the self-assemble DNA sequence is also able to form coacervate of which process is similar to RNA phase separation but could be high ordered folding structures. Although the abovementioned types of phase separation has been modulated as components to reconstitute the phenomenon in synthetic systems, how cells naturally regulate the production of a specific type of coacervate, incorporate certain protein and RNA into droplets without crosstalk, and assemble the droplets spatially still remain unclear. To understand more on the LLPS, it requires to expend the library of phase separation components and reconstitute more complicated systems.

Liquid-to-solid phase separation

On the other hand, liquid-to-solid phase separation is the formation of coacervate which exceeds the critical point on phase diagram. As a result, molecular aggregations might become solid and precipitate as protein cluster. In fact, the intrinsic disordered protein takes part in this process in which the disordered sequence is like a sticker to attach to each others. A well-known example is $\beta$-amyloid which contributes to the progress of alzheimer’s disease. To be detailed, the protein aggregation would accumulate in patient’s brain and trigger a series of aggregation. Nevertheless, the liquid-to-solid phase separation of $\beta$-amyloid is also important to the formation of extracellular matrix, such as biofilm. The precipitate serves as a skeleton to support the grow of the population, project from the toxic and antibiotics and transport signals to each cells. For clinical uses, understanding the process is important to fight against fatal diseases such as alzheimer’s disease, ALS and other amyloid-related diseases. Also, the treatments for the extracellular matrix is useful to eliminate hazard microbe.
